Guidance on some of the most frequent legal situations our chambers handle.

The timeline for a High Court appeal depends on the case type and backlog. High-priority matters like stay orders can be heard quickly, while final arguments for regular appeals may take several months to years depending on complexity.
Anticipatory bail is a discretionary power of the court. The court considers the nature of the accusation, the antecedent of the applicant, and the likelihood of the applicant fleeing from justice. Professional legal drafting is crucial in these matters.
Commonly required documents include Registered Sale Deeds, Mutation details, Tax receipts, Possession certificates, and any previous litigation history related to the property. Complete documentation is key to a solid claim.
